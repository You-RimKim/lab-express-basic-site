About

On January 25, 1964, Lee was born to political dissident parents in Yeongju, Gyeongsangbuk-do, under the rule of President Park Chung-Hee.

During her studies at Hongik University in Seoul, Lee was exposed to conventional mediums: stone and steel forms.

As an independent artist, she worked with softer, more malleable materials, such as fabric, foam, rubber and sequins, then experimented with organic forms, originating from personal perceptions, memories and experiences.

Furthermore, she played with themes of othering, directed from her experiences as a child of dissidents; outward appearances were deemed vital to survival, so Lee was curious about reactions to a grotesque outer shell.
